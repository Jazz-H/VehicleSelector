Cross-Shop

Insight The Ninja 400 / Z400 is the value benchmark — a real step up from your 300F and cheaper to run than the KTM.

Mostly A2-legal, under 600cc, and in reach at $4k cash used — with one over-budget exception, flagged below. Worth a test ride against your shortlist.

Kawasaki Ninja 400 / Z400

The value benchmark — 399cc twin, ~45 hp, standard ABS, and a real step up from your 300F for cheaper running than the KTM. Z400 is the naked, Ninja 400 the faired. Watch: dropped/track bikes; discontinued for 2024 (→ Ninja 500), so hunt the low-mile late ones.

Yamaha MT-03 / R3

The other easy twin — 321cc, ~42 hp, inverted forks + slipper clutch and the lowest, friendliest seat of the group. Smooth and dirt-cheap to own. Watch: mildest power here; some riders want more midrange for the highway.

KTM 390 Duke

The character pick — 373cc single, ~44 hp, sharpest handling and the most attitude of the bunch. Watch: pricier service + parts and faster depreciation than the Japanese twins; confirm ABS is present.

Honda CBR500R

Your CB500F’s faired sibling — identical 471cc twin and running gear, just a fairing and a slight forward lean for wind protection. Same bulletproof reliability. Watch: lean ergonomics if you prefer upright; otherwise a pure ergonomics-vs-CB500F call.

BMW G310 R

Premium badge on a budget — 313cc single, ~34 hp, light and flickable, easy in town. Watch: modest power, early-build fueling quirks/recalls, and a thinner service network than Honda.

Kawasaki Ninja 650 / Z650 — over-budget stretch

The step past the brief — 649cc twin, ~67 hp, more real-world grunt. The flagged exception: it’s over 600cc and usually over $4k. Watch: more bike than a returning rider needs day one; not A2-legal unrestricted.

06

Before You Commit

Insight Rust-bust first, ride a twin vs. a single back-to-back, and quote comprehensive insurance before you buy.
Rust-busting firstIf it's been a while, do a parking-lot refresh (or an MSF Returning Rider course) before highway miles. Your hands remember more than your reflexes — give them a session to catch up.
Singles vs. twinsYour 300F was a single. The CB500 / Ninja 400 / MT-03 are twins — smoother, more top-end, happier on the highway. The Triumph 400 / 390 Duke are punchier singles with more character and vibration. Ride both types back to back — it's the biggest difference in feel.
Ownership costHonda / Yamaha / Kawasaki = cheap parts, long intervals, easy DIY, strong resale. KTM = pricier service and parts, faster depreciation. Factor the real cost, not just the sticker.
Prioritize ABSThe CB300R, CB500s, and Triumph 400 have it standard — just confirm it's present on any KTM or Aprilia you look at.
TheftLight naked/sport bikes are common targets — your cover + lock instincts are right. Add a ground anchor if you park outside, a hidden tracker, and comprehensive insurance so theft is actually covered.
Tire ageCheap used bikes often wear old, hard rubber. Check the 4-digit date code on the sidewall — anything 5+ years old should be replaced regardless of tread.
Season timingIt's peak riding season, so prices are at their highest. If you're not rushing, late fall / winter is when sellers get motivated.
Seat-height fitYou know your comfort range from the 300F. Most of these sit around 31", and the Triumph 400 is the lowest at 31.1" — but always flat-foot a bike before you commit.

Cross-Shop

Insight Buy-once with a Lexus GX470, get a truck with the Colorado or Ridgeline, or go cheap-and-classic with a Cherokee XJ. Each has one known killer to check.

Honorable mentions worth a look depending on your taste and goal.

Lexus GX470

A Land Cruiser under a Lexus badge — V8 body-on-frame 4x4, plush and near-indestructible. The smart buy-once complement. Watch: timing belt (V8), suspension bushings; high miles are fine on these.

Chevy Colorado / GMC Canyon

The cheaper mid-size truck alt to the Tacoma — more truck for the money, still capable. Watch: earlier 5-cylinder quirks; the newer V6 is the one to get.

Honda Ridgeline

The car-based do-everything truck — comfy daily, AWD, clever in-bed trunk. Less trail-hardcore, more genuinely useful. Watch: early transmissions, timing belt — it’s no rock-crawler.

Jeep Cherokee XJ

The OG cheap 4x4 — bulletproof 4.0 straight-six, light, endlessly modifiable, a real trail weapon for little money. Watch: rust (they dissolve) and tired cooling systems.

Ford Bronco (4-door)

The modern off-road icon and the ultimate ’Stang complement — but new-ish and pricey; clean 4-doors run well over budget. Stretch pick if you can swing it.

Ford F-150

Go full-size for max hauling and towing — dirt cheap, endless parts, does truck stuff the Tacoma can’t. Watch: abused work-truck traps; pick the right engine (avoid the early EcoBoost neglect).

// A stretch or priced out: Toyota Land Cruiser, Lexus GX460, Land Rover Defender, Wrangler 392. Great — just not sub-$20k.

06

Before You Commit

Insight Buy the best example, run the VIN, and pick road vs. trail first — rust, a beaten frame, and abused 4x4s are the money pits.
Buy the best exampleA clean $8k car beats a $4k “project” that needs everything. Fixes always run ~2× the estimate — condition and records are the real value.
Rust is the killerCheck frame rails, floors, rockers, arches — and the full frame/undercarriage on the 4x4s. A rust-free needs-mechanical rig beats a clean-running rust bucket. Salt-belt trucks are risky.
Run the VIN + titleCarfax/AutoCheck for accidents, salvage/rebuilt brands, odometer, and liens. Salvage = cheaper but tanks resale and complicates insurance.
Aftermarket + communityA deep parts catalog and an active forum/Discord make or break a build. Avoid orphan platforms where every part is a scavenger hunt.
Mechanical < cosmetic < rustMechanical is the cheapest to fix. Bodywork and rust are the money pits — price accordingly and walk from hidden corrosion.
Skip abandoned buildsHalf-done mods, missing OEM parts, mystery wiring, “ran when parked.” A bone-stock car is usually the smarter, cheaper start.
4x4 done rightOn the Jeep / 4Runner / Xterra, test 4-Hi and 4-Lo (no binding), check diffs and transfer case for leaks, and walk from lift + oversized-tire abuse — a beaten trail rig hides a lot.
Complement, don’t duplicateYour Mustang is the fun RWD 2-door. Buy what it can’t be — 4 doors, 4x4/AWD, weather, cargo, a bed. A second coupe just wastes the slot.
Space, tools, timeBe honest about your garage, your toolset, and your bandwidth. A project you can’t work on becomes a driveway ornament.
Emissions + insuranceModified cars can fail state inspection/emissions — check your rules. And quote insurance first; some of these cost more to cover.
